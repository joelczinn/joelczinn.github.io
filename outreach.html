I've developed two planetarium shows that speak to astronomy's societal significance: one that explores how astronomy has inspired art throughout human history, from Pythagoras to van Gogh; and one that investigates how historical theories of comets, supernovae, and planetary motions may have contributed to the understandings of the Star of Bethlehem.

The show on art and astronomy premiered at the planetarium as part of a larger program of "An evening of astronomy and art", where I supplied art and writing supplies for participants to create their own astronomical masterpieces on the rooftop.

Extending my outreach beyond campus, I organized an open mic night of sorts dubbed "Studio lab": we welcomed artists and scientists alike who had a creative project that they were working on or had finished that was inspired by science.
